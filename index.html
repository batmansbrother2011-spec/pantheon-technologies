The Concept

Simple hardware.
Serious performance.

Under the lid of every MangoDock is a USB-C or Thunderbolt to M.2 adapter — the same core that powers enterprise storage enclosures, priced for everyone.

USB-C → M.2 at the core

Every MangoDock is built around a high-quality USB-C to M.2 bridge chip. Plug in an NVMe or SATA drive you already own and get fast, reliable storage expansion instantly.

Bus-powered

No wall brick needed. The Base draws all its power from your laptop's USB-C port.

Tool-free M.2 swap

Swap drives in seconds. No screwdriver, no fuss — just lift, slide, and go.

FAQ

Common questions

What M.2 drives work with MangoDock Base?

Any M.2 2242 or 2280 drive — both SATA III and NVMe (PCIe Gen 3). You can use a drive you already own or pick up an affordable NVMe SSD. The interface tops out at ~10Gbps so Gen 4 drives will work but won't exceed Gen 3 speeds.

Do I need to install drivers?

On Windows 10 and 11, no drivers are required. macOS and Linux support the standard USB mass storage and USB-C display protocols out of the box. The Pro model may need GPU drivers installed separately.

Can I use any GPU with the Pro model?

Only GPUs that draw 75W or less from the PCIe slot — no power cable required. The best current option is the RTX 3050 6GB (70W, ray tracing, DLSS). The GTX 1650 GDDR6, RX 6400, and Arc A380 also work. RTX 40 and 50 series cards all require external power and are not compatible.

Does the Pro need a power supply unit?

No external ATX PSU is required. The GPU is powered entirely through the PCIe slot, which is why the 75W ceiling exists. The dock itself is powered through Thunderbolt 4 or a small 12V input depending on variant.

Is there a subscription or cloud component?

No. MangoDock is a hardware product. There is no app, no account, no subscription, and no cloud anything. You buy it, you own it.

What's the difference between Thunderbolt 4 and OCuLink on the Pro?

Thunderbolt 4 gives you 40Gbps and works with any modern laptop that has a TB4 port — plug and play. OCuLink provides 64Gbps of raw PCIe bandwidth with lower latency, but requires a reboot when connecting and isn't available on most laptops without an adapter off the M.2 slot. OCuLink is the performance winner; Thunderbolt 4 is the convenience winner.
